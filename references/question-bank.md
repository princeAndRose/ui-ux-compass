# Question Bank

Ask one question at a time. Every question must include why it matters, 2-4 options, and a recommendation.

## Page Role

Question:
What role should this page play in the product?

Why this matters:
The page role determines layout archetype, information density, state coverage, and primary interaction.

Options:

A. Workspace: users repeatedly operate here.
B. Dashboard: users scan status and decide what to do.
C. Editor: users create or modify structured content.
D. Settings: users configure occasionally.
E. Report: users read and evaluate output.

Recommendation:
For high-frequency features, choose A or B. For occasional configuration, choose D.

## Target User

Question:
Who is the primary user for this surface?

Why this matters:
Different users need different density, explanation, defaults, and error recovery.

Options:

A. First-time user who needs orientation.
B. Returning user who wants speed.
C. Expert operator who needs density and control.
D. Decision maker who needs summary and confidence.

Recommendation:
If the product is early, choose B unless the page is onboarding.

## Core Task

Question:
What is the one task this screen must make easy?

Why this matters:
The core task decides P0 hierarchy and prevents equal-weight card soup.

Options:

A. Scan status.
B. Take action.
C. Compare options.
D. Create or edit content.

Recommendation:
Choose the task that should be visible without scrolling.

## First Visual Focus

Question:
What should the user notice first?

Why this matters:
The first visual focus anchors hierarchy, spacing, and CTA placement.

Options:

A. A status summary.
B. A primary work area.
C. A list or table.
D. A primary action.

Recommendation:
Choose the element that best supports the core task.

## Information Density

Question:
How dense should the page feel?

Why this matters:
Density affects spacing, table design, disclosure, and whether helper text helps or clutters.

Options:

A. Low: more explanation and breathing room.
B. Medium: clear but efficient.
C. High: professional tool for frequent use.

Recommendation:
Use B for most MVP product surfaces and C only when users are expert and high-frequency.

## Visual Tone

Question:
What product feel should guide the UI?

Why this matters:
Tone constrains color, typography, component shape, motion, and decorative choices.

Options:

A. Focused tool: utilitarian, dense, calm.
B. Calm personal product: warm, readable, lightly expressive.
C. Data console: precise, structured, comparison-friendly.
D. Branded showcase: memorable and persuasive.

Recommendation:
Choose A for operational products, C for analytics, and D only for marketing-critical pages.

## Acceptance Criteria

Question:
What would make you say yes to this UI?

Why this matters:
Acceptance criteria prevent endless subjective iteration.

Options:

A. Users understand what to do in under 5 seconds.
B. The primary workflow is faster than the current version.
C. The screen feels production-ready with real states.
D. The page communicates value clearly enough to ship publicly.

Recommendation:
Choose A and C for product surfaces; choose D for landing pages.
